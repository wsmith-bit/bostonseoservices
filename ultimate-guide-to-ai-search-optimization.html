3. The Core Pillars of AI Visibility

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO): Make Your Content Easy to Answer

I always recommend structuring your content in a question-and-answer format. Use clear headings, bullet points, and short summaries at the top of each page. Add FAQ sections to address the questions your customers are actually asking.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO): Build Your Authority

Group related content together and link between them. This helps AI see your site as a go-to resource. Use schema markup to tell AI exactly what your content is about—whether it’s an article, a product, or a local business.

Why Internal Linking and Topic Clusters Matter

When I work with clients in Boston, I see how internal linking and topic clusters help AI understand the relationships between different parts of a website. This boosts your authority and makes it easier for AI to recommend your content.

4. Advanced Technical SEO for AI

How AI Crawlers Work

AI crawlers are smarter than traditional search bots. They look for clean, well-structured code and fast-loading pages. If your site is slow or hard to navigate, AI might skip over it.

Try an llms.txt File

You can help AI crawlers by creating an llms.txt file (like robots.txt, but for AI). This tells AI which parts of your site are open for indexing.

Optimize for Voice and Conversational Search

People are asking questions out loud more than ever. I suggest using natural language and anticipating follow-up questions in your content.

Make Sure Your Site Is Mobile-Friendly

AI and users both expect fast, responsive sites. If your site isn’t mobile-friendly, you’re missing out.

5. Website Enhancements for AI Search

Make Your Content Easy to Scan

Use clear headings, break up text with bullet points, and add short summaries at the top of each page. Add FAQ sections and describe your images with alt text.

How to Add TL;DR and FAQs Without a Developer

If you use WordPress, plugins like Schema Pro or Yoast SEO make it easy to add FAQs and summaries. For other platforms, you can usually copy and paste pre-written sections.

Best Practices for On-Page Optimization

Keep your most important content at the top of the page. Use semantic HTML tags like <article> and <section>. Avoid hiding key information behind “Read more” buttons.

6. Schema Markup: The Key to AI Understanding

Why Schema Matters

Schema markup is like a roadmap for AI. It tells AI exactly what your content is about and how it relates to other information.

Types of Schema to Use

  • Article: For blog posts and news.
  • FAQPage: For question-and-answer sections.
  • WebPage: For general pages.
  • LocalBusiness: For businesses with a physical location.
  • Product: For e-commerce sites.
  • CreativeWork: For videos, images, and other media.
  • BreadcrumbList: For navigation.

How to Add Schema to Your Site

If you use Shopify, try apps like JSON-LD for SEO. For WordPress, plugins like Schema Pro, Yoast SEO, or Rank Math work well.

Check Your Schema with These Tools

  • Google Rich Results Test
  • Schema.dev
  • TechnicalSEO.com Tools

7. AI Visibility for Local Businesses

Maximize Your Local Presence

Make sure your Google Business Profile is complete and up to date. Add LocalBusiness schema with your address, hours, and service area. Mention your city and neighborhood in your content and meta titles.

Publish Local FAQs

Answer common questions like “How much does a chiropractor cost in Boston?” This helps AI connect your business to local searches.

Collect Reviews

Ask your customers to mention your services and location in their reviews. This boosts your local authority.

8. AI Visibility for E-commerce

Make Your Products Stand Out

Add Product and Review schema to your product pages. Include FAQs and clear descriptions. Use alt text for your images.

Structured Reviews Help

When I help e-commerce clients, I always recommend adding structured reviews. This helps AI understand what customers think of your products.

9. Advanced Strategies for AI Visibility

Build Your Digital Identity

Use the same name, logo, and description everywhere online. Fill out your profiles on LinkedIn, Crunchbase, About.me, and Google Business Profile.

Entity-Based Schema Markup

Define your business with Organization or LocalBusiness schema. Link to your social profiles with sameAs properties. Connect articles to authors with CreativeWork and Person markup.

Get Cited by Trusted Sources

Being mentioned in local news, review sites, or directories like BBB and Yelp can boost your visibility. Publish thought leadership content and get backlinks to your homepage.

Link Related Entities

Mention and link to your team, services, and partners. Use internal linking to connect service pages to your main business profile.

Build Your Online Footprint

Work with industry blogs and news outlets. If you’re notable, consider building a Wikipedia presence. Engage in community discussions on Reddit and Quora.
