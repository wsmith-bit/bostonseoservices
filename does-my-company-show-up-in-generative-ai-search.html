AEO vs GEO: Understanding the Difference

✅ What is AEO?

AEO focuses on optimizing your content so that AI platforms can present it as a direct answer to user questions.

  • Q&A format: Content structured around questions and answers.
  • Concise summaries: Begin with clear responses (1–2 sentences).
  • Structured data: Use schema, headings, and HTML to organize content.

✅ What is GEO?

GEO ensures your brand is cited in AI-generated summaries, making your business a recognized authority.

  • Entity optimization: Use schema to define your business entity.
  • Topical authority: Provide comprehensive, expert-level content.
  • Trusted citations: Aim for mentions from reputable sources.

Traditional SEO vs AI Search

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking websites in search engines. In contrast, AI search prioritizes answers drawn from optimized content sources.

5 Steps to Appear in AI Search

  1. Research Questions:
    • Use tools: Google People Also Ask, ChatGPT, AlsoAsked.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check if my company appears in ChatGPT answers?

Open ChatGPT and ask: 'What are the best [your service type] in [your city]?' and 'Tell me about [your company name].' Note whether your brand, website, or any details appear. Repeat for Gemini, Perplexity, and Bing Copilot.

What does it mean if ChatGPT doesn't know about my company?

It means your business has weak entity presence in the training data and knowledge sources AI systems rely on. Focus on: (1) complete Google Business Profile, (2) consistent citations across directories, (3) Wikipedia/Wikidata entry if applicable, (4) press mentions on authoritative sites.

Can I pay to appear in AI search results?

Not currently in the same way as paid ads. AI systems cite sources based on relevance, authority, and structured data — not paid placements. Some platforms (like Google's AI Overviews) are beginning to test ad-adjacent placements, but organic optimization remains the primary path.

Does my website need to be large to appear in AI answers?

No. A focused, well-structured site with clear entity information and direct-answer content can outperform large sites. Quality and clarity matter more than size.
